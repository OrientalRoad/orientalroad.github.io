Chongqing

Serving as an economic center in southwestern China, Chongqing is known for its unique mountainous terrain and modern urban development, earning it the nickname "magical city." Videos showcasing its attractions have gone viral on TikTok, making it a popular tourist destination in recent years. Additionally, its cuisine is renowned worldwide, with hot pot being the most well-known dish.

NameChongqing, Chungking
ClimateSubtropical moist monsoon climate

Interests

Travel stories

Morning Flavors in Chongqing
Explore Chongqing's "Five Great" breakfast: tofu soup, youtiao, ciba blocks, glutinous rice balls, and youcha. Taste the local flavors!
Local Flavor & Must-Tries: Chongqing Hot Pot
Discover Chongqing hot pot's history, flavors, and social dining experience. Must-try dishes, varieties, and dipping sauces included. Perfect for gatherings.
Savor Má Là, the Chongqing Jianghu Cuisine
Experience Chongqing Jianghu cuisine, featuring má là (numbing and spicy) flavors. Enjoy chicken dishes, maoxuewang, sour cabbage fish, and local specialties.
Must-Try Chongqing Noddles: Xiaomian
Discover the must-try Chongqing Xiaomian noodles, known for their spicy and numbing flavor. Enjoy variations like minced meat and broad bean paste.
Chongqing's Home Cooking: Sichuan Cuisine
Explore Chongqing's home cooking, a distinct branch of Sichuan cuisine. Taste unique flavors in dishes like douhua congee, yuxiang rousi, and Kung Pao chicken.
Essential Street Eats to Try In Chongqing
Discover must-try Chongqing street foods: fried rice flour triangles, BBQ, sweet potato skins, tofu skin and nian gao. Enjoy local favorites.

Best time to visit

Peak Season

Chongqing is often shrouded in fog during winter, earning it the nickname of "Fog City."
Off-season
